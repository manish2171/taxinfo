India's Great Tax Shift

From Corporate Dominance to Individual Contribution

A Historic Crossover

In a landmark change for India's economy, personal income tax is now the largest source of direct tax revenue.

Personal Income Tax Share (2023-24) ~53% of Total Direct Tax Collections

2004–2009: The Era of Corporate Dominance

In the mid-2000s, India's economic engine was clearly powered by its corporate sector. Corporate tax collections significantly outpaced personal income tax, reflecting a period of robust industrial and business growth.

2014–2019: The Turning Tide

This period marked a critical transition. While corporate tax revenue continued to grow in absolute terms, personal income tax began growing at a much faster rate, narrowing the gap and signaling a fundamental shift in the economy.

Direct Tax Share (2018-19)

Corporate tax's share began to decline as the individual tax base expanded significantly.

2019–2024: A New Paradigm

The shift culminates. Post-2019, personal income tax collections not only caught up but decisively overtook corporate tax. This reflects a more formalized, digitized economy and a broader individual taxpayer base.

Direct Tax Share (2023-24)

Individuals now contribute the majority of direct tax revenue, a complete reversal from a decade prior.

What's Driving the Shift?

The reversal is not accidental but the result of several converging economic and policy factors.

Factors Boosting Personal Tax

  • Broader Tax Base: More individuals entering the formal workforce.
  • Digitization & Compliance: Technology making tax evasion harder and filing easier.
  • Wage Growth: Rising salaries, especially in the services sector, push more people into higher tax brackets.

Factors Moderating Corporate Tax

  • Corporate Rate Cuts: Significant rate reductions in 2019 lowered the relative growth of corporate tax revenue.
  • GST Impact: Corporations benefit from input tax credits under GST, which can reduce their overall tax incidence compared to individuals.
